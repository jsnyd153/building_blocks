• In the wake of the 26th Conference of the Parties, a UN conference on climate change, senior executives can expect more regulations, greater demands for climate-related financial disclosures, and closer scrutiny from investors and customers.

  • Many companies have announced ambitious plans to reduce their emissions, in some cases to net zero, but fewer are making great progress turning those ambitions into action.

  • Four initiatives can help: making the carbon transition integral to strategy, finding ways to monetize investments in carbon reduction, embedding the transition into the business, and engaging middle management.

Runaway emissions. Extreme weather. Food shortages. The story of climate change often feels like one we’re powerless to stop. But we are more powerful than you think.

Together, we can choose a new ending for the story. To do that, a new model of leadership and collaboration is needed, one that demands bold action. If we take hard steps now, if we commit to a more sustainable future for all, we can rewrite the next chapter.

We’re attending the 26th UN Climate Change Conference of the Parties (COP26) in Glasgow to help change the story of climate change.
